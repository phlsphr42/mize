Mize is built for competitive Magic players who are interested in deeper insight within the game. Log your matches, analyze your opening hands, and discover what the data says about the metagame. This is similar to tools that can be found for other games, like HSReplay for Hearthstone players. However, Mize is free!

Entering Game Data

The foundation of Mize is your personal match record. After each match, you log your opening hand along with the results.

Mize walks you through each game in the match. You record which cards were in your opening hand, what the opponent was playing, if the game was a preboard or postboard game, and whether you won or lost. The whole process takes less than a minute per match.

Every match you log contributes to two things: your personal performance history, and the community dataset that powers Mize's analysis features. The opening hand data in particular enables Mize to provide valuable information about which cards tend to comprise the best performing opening hands for the increasingly important opening turns of the game..

Opening Hand Analysis

Once you have enough match data logged, Mize can tell you which cards in your deck are actually making a difference in your win rate.

Bayesian Analysis

Evaluates each card by comparing your win rate in games where the card appeared in your opening hand versus games where it didn't. A Bayesian adjustment accounts for small sample sizes, so cards with only a few appearances are appropriately weighted rather than producing misleading results.

Chi-Squared Analysis

Applies a statistical significance test to the same comparison. Rather than just showing a win rate difference, it tells you how confident you can be that the difference is real and not just variance.

**Highly significant (p < 0.01) — very unlikely to be a coincidence
*Significant (p < 0.05) — probably a real effect
nsNot significant — not enough evidence yet
⚠Insufficient data — sample size too small for meaningful analysis

Meta Matchup Matrix

The Meta Matchup Matrix gives you an overview of how every archetype in the metagame performs against every other archetype.

Each cell shows the win rate of the row deck against the column deck. Green cells indicate a favorable matchup, red cells indicate an unfavorable one. Cells with fewer than 5 games show "—" to indicate insufficient data. Tapping any cell opens a detail view with exact game counts and win rates.

MTGO Deck Rankings

The MTGO Deck Rankings page tracks how every archetype is performing in competitive MTGO events, sourced daily from published decklists and standings.

What the score means

Decks are ranked by an opportunity score that deliberately rewards decks that are outperforming their metagame share. A deck that many players are playing but barely winning with scores lower than a deck that few players are playing but consistently winning. This is designed to reveal potentially underplayed decks that could be strong choices for your next event.

Confidence indicators

High — 100+ appearances: reliable signal
Med — 20–99 appearances: reasonable confidence
Low — fewer than 20 appearances: treat with caution

The vs Field column shows each deck's match win percentage compared to the weighted field average. ▲ indicates above-average performance, ▼ indicates below-average.

Tapping any row opens a full detail view with top 8 conversion rate, average points per event, game win percentage, opponent match win percentage (strength of schedule), and metagame share.

Instead of relying on content creators or social media to tell you what's good, you can see directly what's winning in competitive play, and more importantly, what's winning that not everyone has caught on to yet.

Mize also provides a sort of MTGO Challenge leaderboard. This provides information about the players who are listed in MTGO's publicly published Challenge results and ranks them according to their overall performance, for those who want to separate the hype-generators from the players who are actually consistently showing how things get done.

Your Data

Your personal match history belongs to you. Your individual game records are only visible in your personal analysis views. They are never displayed to other users by name. Community analysis features use aggregated data across all pilots without identifying individuals.

The more matches the community logs, the more powerful the analysis becomes for everyone.
